STUDENT SUPPORT HANDBOOK - SAMPLE DATA

=== ADMISSIONS ===
Admission to the undergraduate program requires a minimum of 60% marks in the qualifying examination (10+2 or equivalent). Applications open in May and close in July each year. Required documents include: 10th and 12th mark sheets, transfer certificate, migration certificate, and passport-size photographs. The application fee is 500 rupees, non-refundable.

Admission to postgraduate programs requires a bachelor's degree with a minimum of 55% marks. Entrance test scores (if applicable) must be submitted along with the application. Merit lists are published on the college website within two weeks of the application deadline.

=== FEE STRUCTURE ===
Tuition fees for undergraduate programs are 45,000 rupees per semester. Postgraduate program fees are 55,000 rupees per semester. Hostel fees (including meals) are 30,000 rupees per semester. Fees can be paid online through the student portal or offline at the accounts office. Late fee payment attracts a fine of 500 rupees per week after the due date.

Scholarships are available for students with family income below 2 lakh rupees per annum, and for students scoring above 90% in their qualifying exam. Scholarship applications must be submitted within the first month of each academic year.

=== EXAMINATION RULES ===
Students must maintain a minimum attendance of 75% in each subject to be eligible to sit for semester examinations. Students with attendance between 65% and 75% may apply for condonation with a valid medical certificate. Students below 65% attendance will not be permitted to appear for exams and must repeat the semester.

Semester examinations are conducted twice a year, in December and May. Internal assessments (assignments, quizzes, mid-terms) contribute 30% to the final grade, while the end-semester exam contributes 70%. Revaluation requests must be submitted within 10 days of result declaration, along with a fee of 200 rupees per subject.

=== HOSTEL AND ACCOMMODATION ===
Hostel accommodation is available on a first-come-first-served basis for outstation students. Rooms are allotted as double-sharing or triple-sharing. Hostel curfew timing is 9:30 PM on weekdays and 11:00 PM on weekends. Visitors are allowed in common areas only, between 4 PM and 7 PM.

Mess facilities provide breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Special dietary requirements (vegetarian, vegan, allergies) should be communicated to the hostel warden in advance.

=== STUDENT SUPPORT SERVICES ===
The Student Counseling Center is open Monday to Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM, offering free confidential counseling for academic stress, personal issues, and career guidance. Appointments can be booked through the student portal.

The Placement Cell assists final-year students with resume building, interview preparation, and connecting with recruiting companies. Placement season typically runs from August to March.

The Library is open from 8 AM to 10 PM on weekdays and 9 AM to 6 PM on weekends. Students can borrow up to 5 books at a time for a period of 14 days. Late return fines are 5 rupees per book per day.

=== GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL ===
Students can submit complaints or grievances through the online Grievance Portal or by contacting the Student Grievance Officer directly. All grievances are addressed within 7 working days. Anti-ragging complaints can be made anonymously through the dedicated anti-ragging helpline.
